May – Aug 2025 News Corp · Remote
AI Product Strategy Extern and Cohort Leader

I was brought on to help figure out why Gen-Z users were disengaging from AI personalization features across News Corp's news apps. As Cohort Leader, I designed and ran a mixed-methods survey coordinated across 50 externs, combining responses to build a statistically meaningful picture of the problem. The analysis surfaced three core barriers: data privacy concerns, perceived information bubbles, and a lack of content transparency. I chose The Times as my focus app, ran a full audit of its current AI features, and developed three specific product recommendations with a phased rollout and A/B testing framework for the engineering team. My submission was selected as the most comprehensive in the cohort, and I was asked to present directly to the VP of Engineering and a senior Product Manager. The team accepted the recommendations and indicated they would move forward with them.

Jan – Mar 2025 PwC · Remote
Non-Profit Consulting Extern

The client was a nonprofit in a tough spot: their donor base was aging, donations were declining, and they were too reliant on individual giving. I started by researching comparable organizations to understand where sustainable long-term funding was actually coming from, then shifted the strategy toward institutional donors. I evaluated over 150 potential funding sources and organized everything in a structured Excel tracker scoring each one on organizational fit, likelihood, application complexity, and timeline. I narrowed the field to three high-potential institutional donors representing up to $1M in funding, built detailed application roadmaps for each, and presented the full strategy to PwC consultants and the client directly. They found it compelling and moved forward with applying to all three.

Ride-Share Pricing Analysis

Merged two large Kaggle datasets in Databricks using Python and SQL to analyze how Uber and Lyft actually structure their pricing. We looked at what variables affect price the most and how both platforms respond to high-demand periods. Linear regression identified surge pricing as the primary driver of fare variance between the two. The end product was a data-backed recommendation for what Lyft would need to change in its pricing and customer trust strategy to close the gap with Uber.
